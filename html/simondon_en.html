The Individual is
an Event.

For 2,000 years, Western thought has been obsessed with the "Brick": the stable, finished unit. We look at the world and see things (a dog, a stone, a citizen).

Simondon reverses the gaze. He asks us to look not at the brick, but at the clay in the kiln. He postulates that stability is just a slow-motion phase of a larger process. To be is not a state, it is an act.

"The individual is not a being, but an act. It is a partial resolution of a pre-individual tension."

Protokin uses Simondon's physics to build a survival manual. If you are not a fixed "thing", you cannot "break". You can only change phase.

Metastability

Stability is death (entropy). Life is "metastable": rich in potential energy, like supercooled water waiting for a shock to freeze. Being alive means being "more than one", holding incompatible tensions together.

Transduction

The Open Object

Against the "Black Box", Simondon praises the "Open Object". A machine must have a "margin of indeterminacy" to be robust. It must allow the human gesture to enter and repair it.

Anxiety as Signal
