# Synthesis — Regime Comparison Matrix

## Protokin Comparative Layer

---

# ⟡ PURPOSE

This document provides a structured comparison of major **descriptive and inferential regimes** analyzed within Protokin.

It does not unify theories.

It maps their **structural differences in terms of Proto / Kin distribution**.

---

# ⟡ DIMENSIONAL FRAME

Each system is analyzed along three axes:

- 🟦 PROTO → stability, constraints, generative structure
- 🟨 KIN → inference, justification, relational structure
- 🟩 COUPLING → interaction between Proto and Kin

---

# ⟡ REGIME MATRIX

| System | Proto (Stability) | Kin (Inference / Relation) | Coupling Type |
|--------|------------------|----------------------------|----------------|
| Active Inference (Friston) | Very high (viability constraints, free energy minimization) | High (Bayesian inference, action-as-inference) | Strong closed-loop coupling |
| Predictive Processing | High (prediction hierarchies, generative models) | High (prediction error updating) | Hierarchical coupling |
| Inferentialism (Brandom) | Medium (implicit social constraints) | Very high (normative inferential structure) | Kin-dominant |
| Classical Symbolic AI | Medium (formal rule systems) | Medium (explicit logical inference) | Weak coupling |
| Dynamical Systems (cognitive science) | Very high (continuous constraints) | Low–Medium (emergent inference) | Proto-dominant |
| Cybernetic Systems (Ashby / Wiener) | High (homeostasis constraints) | Medium (feedback interpretation) | Feedback coupling |

---

# ⟡ STRUCTURAL INTERPRETATION

## 1. Proto-dominant systems

These systems emphasize:

- stability
- constraint satisfaction
- viability conditions

They risk:

> rigidity or under-inferential flexibility

Examples:
- dynamical systems
- biological regulation models

---

## 2. Kin-dominant systems

These systems emphasize:

- inference
- justification
- relational structure

They risk:

> detachment from physical or structural constraints

Examples:
- inferentialism
- normative linguistic systems

---

## 3. Strongly coupled systems

These systems integrate both dimensions:

- stability constrains inference
- inference reshapes stability

Examples:
- active inference
- predictive processing

---

# ⟡ COUPLING TYPES

## 1. Closed-loop coupling
- bidirectional constraint-inference loop
- self-stabilizing systems

## 2. Hierarchical coupling
- layered prediction structures
- top-down / bottom-up interaction

## 3. Weak coupling
- partial independence between structure and inference

## 4. Feedback coupling
- regulatory systems driven by deviation correction

---

# ⟡ TRANSITION LOGIC

A system transitions when:

- Proto layer saturates (instability of constraints)
- Kin layer fails (breakdown of inference validity)
- coupling becomes inconsistent or over-constrained

This produces:

- model revision
- regime shift
- or structural collapse

---

# ⟡ PROTOKIN INTERPRETATION

This matrix is not a classification.

It is a **map of structural tensions** between:

- what stabilizes systems (Proto)
- what makes them meaningful (Kin)

Protokin studies:

> how different configurations of Proto/Kin produce distinct epistemic worlds

---

# ⟡ META-INSIGHT

There is no “optimal” regime.

Each system resolves the Proto–Kin tension differently:

- some prioritize survival (Proto)
- some prioritize justification (Kin)
- some attempt continuous coupling (Hybrid)

---

# ⟡ STATUS

This matrix is:

- a comparative tool
- a structural map
- a synthesis layer of Protokin

It does not reduce differences.

It makes them readable.
