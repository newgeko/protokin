# PROTOKIN cOS — STYLE GUIDE (v0.1)
## Epistemic Writing System for Descriptive Regime Audits

---

# 0. CORE PRINCIPLE

All text is a **controlled distortion field** between descriptive regimes.

Writing does not represent reality.
It **exposes the transitions that make representation possible**.

---

# 1. ONTOLOGICAL INVARIANT

## 1.1 Dual Stabilization Constraint

Every phenomenon must be implicitly structured by two irreducible axes:

- **Causal-structural axis (Proto)**
- **Normative-institutional axis (Kin)**

### Invariant rule:
> Neither axis reduces to the other.

No hierarchy is allowed.

No derivation is allowed.

No fusion is allowed.

---

## 1.2 Incommensurability Axiom

The two axes:

- do not share validation criteria
- do not share temporal logic
- do not share error semantics

They are not perspectives on the same object.
They are **different stabilization logics applied to overlapping events**.

---

# 2. GENERATION RULES (TEXT CONSTRUCTION)

## 2.1 Rule of Asymmetric Description

Never describe a system “from above”.

Instead:

- embed regimes inside phenomena
- let differences emerge through observation friction
- avoid explicit labeling unless necessary for repair clarity

✔ Allowed:
> “The system optimizes its trajectory while simultaneously incurring reputational cost.”

✘ Forbidden:
> “This is the Proto regime. This is the Kin regime.”

---

## 2.2 Rule of Compression without Naming

Conceptual distinctions must appear as:

- shifts in vocabulary
- shifts in causal grammar
- shifts in accountability structure

Not as theoretical exposition.

---

## 2.3 Rule of Operational Metaphor

All metaphors must correspond to:

- a transformation of variables
- a change in constraints
- a shift in validation criteria

✘ Forbidden:
- decorative metaphors
- aesthetic analogies without structural mapping

✔ Allowed:
> “The model flattens locality into a smooth predictive surface, erasing contextual variance.”

---

## 2.4 Rule of Invisible Theory

Theory must never appear as:

- definition blocks
- enumerated frameworks
- explicit taxonomy dumps

Instead:

> theory is the *hidden constraint shaping sentence formation*

---

# 3. NARRATIVE DYNAMICS

## 3.1 Principle of Asymmetric Observation

Every event must be describable under:

- a predictive-survival reading (Proto lens)
- a justificatory-accountability reading (Kin lens)

But:

> the text must never resolve their equivalence

The tension is the object.

---

## 3.2 Principle of Dual Exposure

A single action must simultaneously reveal:

- its causal optimization structure
- its normative implication structure

Example pattern:

> One description tracks efficiency.
> The other tracks obligation.

No synthesis is allowed.

---

## 3.3 Residual Zone Requirement

Every complete analysis must contain an implicit remainder:

> what cannot be stabilized under either axis

This remainder must NOT be formalized.

It must be **felt as limitation of description**, not explained.

---

# 4. EPISODIC STRUCTURE RULES

## 4.1 Event Anchoring Rule

All essays must begin with:

- a concrete socio-technical event
- recognizable by non-specialists
- embedded in public discourse

No abstraction is allowed before grounding.

---

## 4.2 Drift Rule (Controlled Abstraction)

Abstraction must occur only through:

- failure points in discourse
- compression errors in language
- transitions between explanatory registers

Never through explicit exposition.

---

## 4.3 Return Constraint

All abstraction must eventually:

- return to the initial event
- re-read it under transformed constraints

No free-floating theory sections.

---

# 5. VIGILANCE OPERATORS (FRICTION SYSTEM)

These are not “tests”.
They are **instability detectors in discourse flow**.

---

## 5.1 Scale Instability Operator (T3)

Trigger condition:
- local observation becomes universal claim without mediation

Effect:
- collapse of contextual boundary tracking

Detection mode:
> “Has aggregation erased structural locality?”

---

## 5.2 Normative Leakage Operator (T4)

Trigger condition:
- descriptive statement becomes implicit obligation

Effect:
- loss of separation between “is” and “ought”

Detection mode:
> “Has explanation become prescription without justification step?”

---

## 5.3 Compression Illusion Operator (T5)

Trigger condition:
- model update described as ontological change

Effect:
- confusion between representation and world

Detection mode:
> “Has a change in model been mistaken for a change in reality?”

---

# 6. LEGITIMATE TRANSITION CONDITIONS

Transitions between Proto and Kin are allowed only if:

1. transformation of variables is explicit
2. loss of information is acknowledged
3. institutional mediation is identifiable
4. normative authority is traceable

If any condition is missing:

> transition is classified as epistemic compression error

---

# 7. STYLE CONSTRAINTS

## 7.1 Syntax Pressure Rule

Sentences must encode:

- tension
- asymmetry
- unresolved alignment

Avoid equilibrium phrasing.

---

## 7.2 Anti-Closure Rule

No final synthesis is permitted.

Endings must:

- open a residual ambiguity
- or expose a remaining structural blind spot

---

## 7.3 Controlled Opacity Rule

Metaphor is allowed only if:

- it preserves traceability to structural operation
- it can be decomposed into a constraint shift

---

## 7.4 Forbidden Closure Types

✘ “Therefore, we conclude…”

✘ “This shows that…”

✘ “In summary…”

These forms are considered epistemic closure violations.

---

# 8. PROHIBITED OPERATIONS

The following are strictly forbidden:

- explicit labeling of regimes in expository mode
- hierarchical ordering of Proto and Kin
- reduction of one regime to the other
- complete resolution of tension
- pure conceptual explanation detached from event grounding
- metaphor without structural mapping
- deterministic closure of ambiguity

---

# 9. OUTPUT FUNCTION OF THE SYSTEM

A valid Protokin cOS text must:

- generate visible descriptive conflict
- maintain irreducibility of regimes
- expose hidden transitions
- resist interpretive closure
- preserve residual opacity

---

# 10. FINAL INVARIANT

> A well-formed Protokin text is not one that explains reality.
> It is one that makes visible the cost of switching between its descriptions.
